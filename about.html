About Me

I am a computer science major at Dartmouth, with a motley assortment of interest, activities, and goals. Computer science has been the root of my personality (dorky) and the source of my aspirations (lofty) since I was 10 and first realized that someone out there had to have known how to make computers and tell them what to do, even if I wasn't quite sure how. Sometimes I'm still not sure how. Luckily, I can, and will, learn. I like to focus on continual, fast-paced improvement of my skills without focusing too much on my failures, which I'm sure there will be many more of. I strive to not only be a better programmer, but also a better person. At the end of the day why wouldn't you be both?

I've been pursuing this major for all of my time thus far at Dartmouth, and have coded in Java, Javascript, C, and Python. More recently, I have been taking an Algorithms class with Dr.Thomas Cormen, who is, as he will tell you, one of the four co-authors of the famous CLRS Intro to Algorithms textbook. While challenging, I find that the problems posed in that class have sparked my creativity in a way that I feel no other subject does. As always, I find the subject matter challenging, but rewarding. My primary interest now is to apply my computer science knowledge to make something with real-world impact outside of class.

What I Do

While a majority of my time is spent in the library, when I do get out I have a few other primary interests. I am on the executive board of Club Biathlon and the Dartmouth Mountaineering Club, both sports I enjoy. Besides this, I am active in the Women in Computer Science club on campus, and enjoy both studio art and photography.
